The Role

Personal Carer &
Assistant in Nursing

Imagine being able to positively impact someone's life by helping them with their shopping, preparing meals, assisting with personal care, cleaning their homes, driving them to appointments or monitoring medication. Be it through helping with problem resolution, lending a listening ear over a cup of tea or cleaning up to provide a safe environment, your desire to make a difference in the lives of our customers will shine through.

Please refer to the services section of our website (www[dot]careforaustralia[dot]com/services) to understand what types of services we offer to our clients and thus, what your roles will be.

Registered Nurses

RNs will deliver clinical care and services in areas such as (depending on the RN's experience): chronic disease management, wound care, continence care, bowel and bladder management, implementing or monitoring care plans, assisting with oxygen and/or eteral feeding, supervision of medication, monitoring vital signs, help with medical equipment, scheduling doctors appointment, assistance with self medication, catherer care, palliative care and pre & post-acute hospital care.

RNs may also provide specialised care (depending on the RN's experience in the field): Disability (Vision Impairment, Hearing Impairment, Intellectual Disabilities); Mental Health (Anxiety, Depression); Aged-Care Specific (Dementia, Parkinson's Disease), and Chronic Medical Diseases (Arthritis, Asthma, Cardiovascular Disease and Diabetes).

We would like to hear from you if you are a kind and loving person and aspire to join us on our mission to change the aged care and disability industries for the better.

Eligibility

To qualify for the Personal Carer or Assistant-in-Nursing Position, applicants must:
  • Have a certificate III or IV, bachelor's or master's degree in aged care, disability, nursing, healthcare or medicine (see note 1 below);

  • Have at least one year of paid or unpaid work experience (see note 2 below);

  • Have a valid Driver's license and be able to travel to clients' homes;

  • Have a police check conducted in the past 12 months; and

  • Be physically capable of assisting clients with mobility, including lifting and transferring.
To qualify for the Registered Nurse Position, applicants must:
  • Have a Bachelor of Nursing and/or Master of Nursing;

  • Have experience in the specific services that are required by the client (this varies based on the client);

  • Is currently registered with AHPRA;

  • Have at least one year of work experience;

  • Have a valid Driver's License and be able to travel to clients' homes;

  • Have a police check conducted in the past 12 months; and

  • Be physically capable of assisting clients with mobility, including lifting and transferring.
